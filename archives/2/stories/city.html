chug

At night in the heat

I dream of all the tattoos I would have

If I were a substrate for paintings

My friend ebbs in and out of color

Like a neon fish in a mourning veil

Asking me how I'm doing

While we dug theoretical gardens

For sprawling lavender ivy and fairy nectars,

A deer ate our one tomato plant

Everything in the fridge is getting solid

But we are vapors that can't cool into water

Until the rent is payed


sheep

The chickens and goats we don't have

write things on my chest for half the day.

Swearwords, mostly.

Playground insults

And shit graffitti.

In the mornings you draw them out with honey salve

And wet matches

And kisses their foreheads till they sleep.

I do the dishes wrong sometimes.

Right other times.


THURSDAY

I caught my own tail today.

It was gross

The blood matted in my jaw.

We turned the music up louder.

So we wouldn't think about it.

I thought about it.

Couldn't do the extraction steady

So when it finally got loose,

It took wisdom teeth with it.


room

The patchwork cat of our house

Who's been here long before we were

Rolls the blinds in at midnight

And lays a spell down.

We sleep insulated

Protected

The world rattles around us

But nestled in the triangle

Three cities locking arms with each other

And surrounded by nothing but green

And disinterested highway

We barely notice our teacups shake.

We debate ginger and earl grey in quiet voices

While our friends forget we're here.


afternoon

Hollow waisted and smooth

Auburn

Standing

In a softglow dream she births a fox

Dripping

Matted

Wriggling from her chest

To arms

Sleepy

Dewey wonder she mouths words

Assessments

Compromises

Thought aloud into the caverns

Of kitchen floor

Of afternoon

Of gently rocking

and mewling housecats

"Say Hello"

She says

"This is the year two thousand and sixteen"
